Climbing Suilven

Norman MacCaig

I nod and nod to my own shadow and thrust A mountain down and down. Between my feet a lock shines in the brown, Its silver paper crinked and edged with rust. My lungs say No; But down and down this treadmill hill must go.

Parishes dwindle. But my parish is This stone, that tuft, this stone And the cramped quarters of my flesh and bone. I claw that tall horizon down to this; And suddenly My shadow jumps huge miles away from me.
